Our Story

How Daily Spark was born

Daily Spark started with a simple observation: millions of Indian students, job seekers, and families search for reliable information every day — about JEE admissions, government jobs, health tips, recipes — but end up lost in cluttered, ad-heavy, unreliable sites.

"What if one platform gave every Indian the knowledge they need, clearly, honestly, and in their own language?"

That question became Daily Spark. We started as a small project focused on JoSAA and JAC Delhi counselling guidance for B.Tech aspirants, helping students navigate one of the most confusing processes of their academic lives.

Today, Daily Spark has grown into a multi-topic knowledge platform covering Education, Government Jobs, Health & Wellness, Cooking, Current Affairs, Weather, and more — all with the same commitment to clarity and trust that we started with.

We are proudly Made in India, built by young creators who understand what Indian readers need, and dedicated to keeping all our content permanently free.

How We Work

The principles we never compromise on

1
Accuracy First
Every fact we publish is checked. We would rather say less and be right than say more and mislead. Our readers trust us — and we take that seriously.
2
Always Free
Daily Spark will never charge readers for access. Knowledge is a right, not a subscription. Our content stays free, forever — supported responsibly by advertising.
3
Reader-First Design
We design for clarity, not clicks. No misleading headlines, no sensationalism — just information presented so clearly that even a first-time reader can act on it.
4
Bilingual at Heart
India speaks many languages. Daily Spark speaks Hindi and English — because knowledge loses its power when it is only available to those who read one language.
5
Privacy Respected
We collect only what we need and never sell your data. Your trust is more valuable to us than any data point. Read our Privacy Policy.
6
Continuously Improving
We are a growing platform. We listen to feedback, fix mistakes promptly, and keep adding content that genuinely serves our readers' lives — not just our metrics.
